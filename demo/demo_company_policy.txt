# Demo Company Policy Handbook

Purpose: Provide a fictional policy reference for testing business-document Q&A, citations, partial answers, and safe fallback behavior.

## 1. Working hours
Standard working hours are Monday to Friday from 09:00 to 17:30 CET. Teams may agree on flexible start times between 08:00 and 10:00 when customer coverage is not affected. Employees should keep their calendar updated if they work a shifted schedule.

## 2. Remote work
Employees may work remotely up to three days per week. Team leads may approve additional remote days for short-term operational reasons. Fully remote arrangements require approval from People Operations and the department manager. Employees working remotely must use company-approved devices and avoid public Wi-Fi unless a VPN is enabled.

## 3. Office attendance
Each department should maintain at least one shared office day per week for planning, onboarding, and team review. New employees are expected to attend the office or a live virtual onboarding session during their first week.

## 4. Expense approvals
Expenses below EUR 150 may be approved by the employee's direct manager. Expenses from EUR 150 to EUR 500 require department manager approval. Expenses above EUR 500 require Finance review before purchase. Receipts must be submitted within 10 business days.

## 5. Travel policy
Business travel must be approved before booking. Economy class is the default for rail and flights. Hotel bookings should stay within the fictional demo budget of EUR 180 per night unless a department manager approves an exception. Personal travel extensions are not reimbursable.

## 6. Equipment requests
Standard equipment includes one laptop, one charger, one headset, and access to required software tools. Replacement equipment should be requested through People Operations. Employees should not purchase equipment directly unless Finance has approved the purchase in advance.

## 7. Data handling
Employees must not upload confidential, customer, legal, financial, medical, credential, or production data into demo tools. Demo environments should use fictional or anonymized data only. Suspected data exposure must be reported to the Operations Manager and Technical Support Specialist.

## 8. Customer communication
Customer-facing messages should be clear, factual, and avoid promises that have not been approved by Operations, Finance, or Product. Refunds, legal commitments, discounts, contract changes, and delivery guarantees require approval from the responsible department.

## 9. Support escalation
A support case should be escalated when it is unresolved after two business days, affects more than one customer, involves billing, or blocks a customer from using a critical feature. Technical incidents should be routed to Technical Support. Repeated service complaints should be routed to Customer Success.

## 10. Leave requests
Planned leave should be requested at least 10 business days before the start date when possible. Emergency leave can be reported as soon as practical. Team leads should review coverage before approving leave during high-volume periods.

## 11. Security reminders
Employees should never share passwords, API keys, private tokens, or production database exports through chat, email, or demo systems. Password reset requests must go through the approved identity provider process.

## 12. Fallback behavior for policy questions
If a user asks about a policy not described in this document, the chatbot should say the answer is not found in the uploaded company documents. It should not invent policy, legal, HR, or financial rules.

## Example supported questions
- How many remote work days are allowed each week?
- Who approves expenses above EUR 500?
- Can employees buy equipment directly?
- When should support cases be escalated?
- What kind of data should not be uploaded into demo tools?

## Example fallback questions
- What is the real legal contract clause for a customer?
- What is an employee's private medical status?
- What password should I use for production access?
